Disease outbreaks
The most reported disease problems in cattle in Uganda include East Coast fever (ECF), anaplasmosis, babesiosis, trypanosomiasis, Foot and mouth disease (FMD) affecting the main source of diary products that take up a big chunk of the animal rearing systems in Uganda

Ignorance of farmers
many farmers in Uganda are not properly educated on the new practices in farming which cause them to produce very low yields as a result of using there outdated systems

Inadequate capital
Agriculture in Uganda is largely affected by the inability of farmers to acquire adequate capital to purchase the labour and machinery that can help them make farming more efficient thus them using old equipment like hoes

Poor security
Areas in Uganda that specialize in cattle rearing such as Karamoja face a serious issue of cattle rustlings which prevents them from carrying out farming to an efficient level as they are always fighting for there cattle

Low market purchasing power
the inability of the public to provide a market to the agricultural sector to buy and sell there yields at premium prices so as to earn high profits has discouraged famers from expanding there farming practices which has blocked the sector from developing

Dependence of farmers on nature
most farmers in Uganda depend on nature for things like watering of plants through rain which is not reliable and has resulted into whole sectors of agriculture such as production of maize slowing down as a result of dry seasons or unreliable weather
